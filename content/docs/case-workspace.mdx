---
title: Case search, list & detail
description: Configure how people find, compare, and open cases in the Nova builder.
---

The case workspace controls how people find a case, compare the results, and
understand the case they open. It is split into three focused tabs so you can
work on one part of that experience at a time.

## Three focused tabs

| Tab | What you configure |
| --- | --- |
| **Search** | The questions people use to narrow the case list, including their labels and input types. |
| **List** | The information people scan while choosing a case. |
| **Detail** | The information people see after opening a case. |

The count on each tab includes only the fields visible on that screen. A field
that is useful behind the scenes does not inflate the visible field count.

## Visible and supporting fields

The List and Detail canvases show only what the person using the app will see.
Fields that are not shown on the current screen are collected under
**Supporting fields** instead of being mixed into the visible layout. A
supporting field may still appear on the other screen, sort the case list, or
support app logic.

Select any field to edit it in the right sidebar. The **Shown on** settings are
the one place to choose whether it appears in the case list, the case detail,
both, or neither. When neither is selected, the field stays available as a
supporting field without becoming a visible row or column.

## One field order, shared by both screens

The case list and case detail share one field order. Choose **Arrange fields**
from either tab to open **Field order** in the right sidebar, then drag the full
stack into place. Supporting fields keep their position in the stack, so a
field appears in the expected place if you show it later.

Field order controls where information appears. **Sort order** is separate: it
controls which cases appear first in the list.

## Preview without starting over

The edit canvases can show values from your real case data while you configure
the layout. To try the complete experience, use **Preview** in the builder
header. Preview runs the actual search, list, and detail screens against the
same case data.

Nova keeps the running preview in its flipbook. Switch back to edit a setting,
then return to Preview and continue where you left off instead of restarting
the app and retracing every step.

## Working with the sidebars

The workspace is designed to remain readable with both sidebars open. Use the
left sidebar to keep your place in the app and the right sidebar for chat or
the settings of the selected item. You can collapse either sidebar when you
want more room; the canvas reflows without moving a setting to a different
place.
